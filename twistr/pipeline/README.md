# `twistr.pipeline` — Lead-optimization model for helix-mediated binder design

This module is the geometry-and-contact predictor at the core of an
agentic binder-design pipeline targeting GI indications. Initial helix
candidates are generated by PXDesign + Boltz; an agent then iteratively
mutates them and scores each proposal against three downstream metrics:
**shape complementarity (SC), electrostatic complementarity (EC), and
buried surface area (BSA)** against the target. This model is what the
agent calls on every proposed mutation to predict the full-atom helix
structure and the residue-pair interaction pattern from which SC, EC,
and BSA are computed.

## Design intent

The agent's inner loop dictates four hard constraints:

1. **One-shot, no recycling, no diffusion.** Every proposed mutation
   needs a forward pass; an 8× recycle or a 50-step diffusion sample is
   not practical at agent-loop frequency.
2. **Mutation-sensitive predictions.** Sequence is the agent's primary
   degree of freedom — the predicted interaction matrix has to respond
   to single-residue substitutions in a locality-respecting way, or the
   agent's gradient signal vanishes.
3. **Faithful heavy-atom geometry.** SC and BSA are computed on
   predicted atom-14 coordinates, so the supervised objective targets
   per-channel contact geometry (VDW band, aromatic stacking sub-types,
   h-bond donor-acceptor geometry) and hydrophobic-packing density
   directly — not only fold-level RMSD.
4. **In-distribution generalization.** Inference is on agent-proposed
   point mutations of known-binding helices; the manifold the model
   sees in production is the manifold of helix-mediated interfaces.
   Cluster-level OOD generalization is explicitly not a goal.

## Position in the landscape

- **RFdiffusion / Chroma / FrameDiff:** generative, structure-first, de
  novo. Used (via PXDesign / Boltz) at the *initial proposal* step.
  Great at generating plausible folds; less suited to iteration against
  a fixed downstream metric.
- **ProteinMPNN:** sequence-from-structure. Orthogonal — sequence is
  this model's input, not output.
- **AlphaFold 2/3, Boltz, Chai-1:** general-purpose structure prediction
  with recycling. Used as oracles to spot-check designs; too slow and
  too generic for the agent's inner loop.
- **BindCraft, AlphaProteo:** end-to-end binder design pipelines.
  Comparable in scope, but built around their own structure predictors;
  this model fills the analogous inner-loop predictor role inside our
  pipeline.

## Architecture

A four-block AF3-style **Pairformer** trunk feeds three output heads:

- a **frame head** producing the 6D rotation parameterization of Zhou
  et al. 2019 plus a translation delta, both as *residuals on the
  per-residue conditioning frame* — partner residues land at their GT
  backbone pose at step 0, helix residues at the canonical-layout pose
  at the origin;
- a **torsion head** emitting seven L2-normalized (sin, cos) pairs per
  residue in AF2 rigid-group ordering [ω, φ, ψ, χ₁..χ₄];
- a **6-channel interaction-matrix head** with per-channel logit biases
  initialized to the prior positive rate of each contact class
  (interfaces are sparse; starting at sigmoid(0)=0.5 wastes the first
  ~1k steps learning the prior).

Predicted atom-14 coordinates are composed by applying the predicted
(R, t, χ) to AF2's canonical chi=0 reference layout, with AF2's
rigid-group constants extracted at module load via AST from the
AlphaFold submodule — no copy-paste, no JAX import.

## Training signal

Multi-objective: a coordinate anchor (atom-14 MSE in Å²), 6-channel
BCE on the interaction matrix (multi-label, channels independent), and
nine differentiable physical priors selected to support the agent's
downstream metrics:

| Agent metric | Loss(es) targeting it |
|---|---|
| Buried surface area | `coord_mse_annealed`, `packing` |
| Shape complementarity | `packing`, `vdw`, aromatic stacking sub-types, `clash` |
| Electrostatic complementarity | `hbond` geometric, `interaction_bce` (h-bond channel) |
| Rotamer plausibility | `dunbrack` (joint vMM) |
| Local backbone integrity | `helix_dihedral`, `backbone_continuity` |

Per-loss weights are routed through a shared linear-ramp helper that
implements a **handoff schedule**: the coordinate anchor dominates the
gradient early (real protein geometry trivially satisfies the band
losses); physical priors are ramped up in lockstep as MSE anneals down.
We surfaced and fixed an empirical artifact where heavy-atom geometry
drifted out of the VDW band as MSE released without geometric priors
compensating; the handoff infrastructure addresses this directly.

## Selected technical contributions

- **Mutation-sensitivity diagnostic.** Per val example, sample K random
  single-residue helix substitutions, hold the conditioning fixed, and
  measure how much the predicted IM probabilities shift relative to
  wild-type — split into "local" (pairs within a same-chain residue-
  index radius of the mutation) and "far". `local > far` is the signal
  we want. This diagnostic surfaced a load-bearing missing s→z coupling
  in the input embedder (AF3 Algorithm 2): without the row+column
  projections of `s` into `z`, residue-type information never reaches
  `z` and the IM head's predictions are identically mutation-invariant.
- **Conditioning interaction matrix as soft contact prior.** The
  partner structure goes in as coordinates; the contact pattern goes
  in as a noised (per-example bit flips + per-cell Beta sampling), soft
  (B, N, N, 8) tensor with explicit augmentation-mask and padding-mask
  channels. The agent can prescribe contact intent ("I want a hot-spot
  here") directly through the input rather than infer it from structure.
- **Joint von-Mises Dunbrack rotamer prior.** Standard practice is per-
  chi 1D vMMs; we fit a joint n_χ-dimensional vMM per (residue,
  secondary-structure class) from the quality-filtered (RSPERC ≥ 25)
  Shapovalov & Dunbrack 2011 dataset, with explicit π-periodic
  augmentation for the four 2-fold-symmetric chi axes (ASP χ₂, GLU χ₃,
  PHE χ₂, TYR χ₂). Captures χ–χ correlations factorized priors miss.
  Loss-time evaluation symmetrizes the predicted χ under the same flip,
  making the loss *exactly* invariant under χ_c → χ_c + π regardless
  of any EM convergence asymmetry.
- **Packing-neighbor loss for SC and BSA.** For each interface
  residue's aliphatic stub atoms and aromatic ring atoms, counts
  heavy-atom neighbors in the VDW band on residues with supervised
  positions, penalizes under-packing via `relu(n_target − count)`.
  Neighbor scope is restricted to atoms anchored by another loss
  (helix all-atoms, antigen backbone, antigen interface sidechains) so
  the model can't fake packing against unconstrained drifted
  sidechains. Directly targets the metrics the agent scores against.
- **Helix-only gating** on backbone continuity. The antigen comes in
  as a contact-radius crop with arbitrary chain breaks at the
  boundaries; AF2-style peptide-bond geometry across a crop break is
  meaningless. The continuity loss is gated on both residues being
  helix; antigen pairs contribute zero.
- **Sequence-disjoint validation.** The val set holds out helix
  sequences pairwise unique and absent from train. Examples whose
  sequence is chosen for val but which are not the single representative
  are dropped from both sides (~1.5K of 424K) to maintain both
  invariants. Required for the mutation-sensitivity metric to be
  meaningful.

## Evaluation

- **Coordinate accuracy** on the sequence-disjoint val split (per-atom
  MSE in Å², backbone vs sidechain).
- **Per-channel BCE / geometric loss** on the val interaction matrix.
- **Mutation sensitivity** (above); current production runs maintain
  `local > far` consistently across training.
- **Planned:** SC / EC / BSA correlation against a held-out set of
  experimentally validated binders; agreement of predicted ΔΔG with
  scanning-mutagenesis data; AF3 callback success rate on top-scoring
  agent designs.
